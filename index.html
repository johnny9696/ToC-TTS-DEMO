Abstract


As human-computer interaction becomes increasingly important, synthesizing human-like speech has become a challenging issue. Early neural text-to-speech models focused on generating pronunciation, making it difficult to synthesize appropriate speech based on the situation. Previous research on conversational TTS focused on understanding the whole conversation without considering the speaker's relationships or emotions. They also used pretrained models to extract the feature vector of text and audio, which could cause a mismatch between the ground truth feature vector and the synthesized speech feature vector. To address these problems, we proposed a text-only conversational text-to-speech model that contains two modules. First, the text-only dialogue context encoder provides conversation context based on understanding the conversation flows and the speaker's relationships. This module uses only text, which could result in a lack of information compared to the multi-modal context model. To solve this problem, we developed a target prosody extractor that extracts prosody based on ground truth speech. This allows the text-only dialogue context encoder to predict prosody. Our proposed model reached a MOS score of \blue{4.xx}, demonstrating its naturalness and quality in synthesizing speech based on dialogue.

3. Evaluation Results

    Mean Opinion Score(MOS) and Dialogue Mean Opinion Score (D-MOS) was rated from 1 to 5. And average all the score with 95% confidence rate.

    Log-F0 Root Mean Square Error (F0 RMSE) is the pitch accuracy. Pitch was extracted by pyworld library, and calculated into log scale. Then calculate with root mean square between ground truth and synthesized speech.

    Mel Ceptrial Distortion (MCD) calculate the difference between ground truth audio and synthesized speech.
